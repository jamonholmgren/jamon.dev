What I use

Hardware

Here's a list of the hardware I use on a day-to-day basis. Note some links are affiliate links because ... well, I'll use the minor amount of revenue to buy more hardware, I guess.

  • Main Computer: 2022 Mac Studio M1 Max

    This is my main driver and what I'm editing this website on right now. It's been a great computer! Quiet and powerful.

  • Secondary Computer: 2022 Eluktronics MECH-17 Grand Performance

    I really like this small, scrappy, DIY-friendly computer maker out of New Jersey. They primarily focus on gaming laptops. This is the second gaming laptop I've bought from them, and it's awesome -- comes with liquid cooling, even!

  • Display: LG UltraFine 5k Display

    I use my primary computer in clamshell mode, so this is my primary display. It's a 5k display, which is a lot of pixels. It's also expensive. I sort of wish I had one of those huge curved displays, but this is pretty amazing in its own right.

  • Keyboard: Microsoft Sculpt Ergonomic Keyboard

    I used the Apple Magic Keyboard for a while, but my hands weren't particularly comfortable. I generally like the Sculpt and would likely buy it again.

  • Trackpad: Apple Magic Trackpad

    I don't really use a mouse these days, except for gaming, and for work I use the Magic Trackpad. It does the job and is pretty solid overall.

  • Mouse: Anker 2.4G Wireless Vertical Ergonomic Optical Mouse

    I don't use a mouse very often, but when I do, it's this ergonomic vertical mouse. Pretty inexpensive and does the job.

  • Headphones: Sennheiser Pro Audio HD280PRO Headphone

    I podcast and stream, so I need decently nice headphones. These are comfortable and don't bleed through sound to the microphone.

  • Microphone: Electro-Voice RE320 and Broadcast Arm

    This is a fantastic microphone for my podcasting and streaming. It sounds amazing and the arm makes it very convenient to push out of the way when I'm not using it.

  • Mixing Console: Yamaha AG03 Mixing Console

    This mixing console allows me to connect the microphone to my computer and also allows for adjusting volume, monitor (which I use) and other stuff I don't understand. It's a great piece of equipment, albeit takes up a bit of desk space.

  • Desk: Zinus Alicia Industrial Style [Desk]

    I have long legs and prefer a deep desk. This is technically a dining table, but I use it as a desk and it looks and works great. The only issue is that the table surface is slightly rough/uneven. However, with the desktop pad (below), I don't notice it.

  • Desk Pad: TOWWI Dual Sided Desk Pad

    This is one of my favorite desk accessories. It pads the underside of my keyboard and lets me slide everything under my custom-built desk shelf in one go.

  • Chair: IKEA Markus

    Call me basic, but I honestly like this chair. It fits my body pretty well and I can sit on it for hours.

Please note that some of the product links are affiliate links.
